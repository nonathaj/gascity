Gas City orchestrates fleets of coding agents through real engineering work.
You write a **formula** — a method for how a job gets done — and the
**controller** runs it as a graph: it decomposes the job into beads, fans the
ready ones out to many agents at once, gates each step until its dependencies
close, retries what fails, drains convoys in parallel, and drives the whole
graph to completion *outside your session*. [Orders](/tutorials/07-orders)
trigger formulas on a schedule or event; health patrol keeps the fleet alive.
This orchestration is the point.

Gas City is built from six primitives:

![The six primitives and how they relate: Packs declare agents, formulas, and orders; a Formula operates over a convoy of Beads, fanning work out to Agents that execute in a Rig; Events are fired as it happens so humans and agents can observe.](diagrams/excalidraw-rendered/primitives.svg)

- **Agent** — *who* does the work (sessions, providers, and pools derive under it)
- **Bead** — *what* the work is
- **Formula** — *how* a job gets done, the method the controller runs as a graph across many agents
- **Rig** — *where* it happens, a project registered with the city
- **Pack** — what *configures* the system; the City is the local (root) pack and imports shared packs
- **Event** — fired by activity so you can *observe* what happened

model, then read the [Architecture Overview](/concepts/architecture-overview)
to see how they fit together and how work flows through them.
